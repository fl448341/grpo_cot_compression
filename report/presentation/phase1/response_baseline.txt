Problem (answer 27): Morisette and Kael were asked to bring fruits. Morisette brought 5 apples and 8 oranges, while Kael brought twice the amount of apples
and half the number of oranges than Morisette. How many fruits do they have in total?

[baseline / empty prompt | 302 tok | correct]
To determine the total number of fruits that Morisette and Kael brought, we need to calculate the number of each type of fruit separately and then sum them up.

First, let's find out how many apples Kael brought:
Kael brought twice the amount of apples as Morisette. Since Morisette brought 5 apples, Kael brought \(2 \times 5 = 10\) apples.
Next, let's find out how many oranges Kael brought:
Kael brought half the number of oranges as Morisette. Since Morisette brought 8 oranges, Kael brought \(\frac{8}{2} = 4\) oranges.
Now, we can calculate the total number of fruits that Kael brought:
\[10 \text{ (apples)} + 4 \text{ (oranges)} = 14 \text{ fruits}\]
Since Morisette also brought 8 oranges, the total number of fruits that Morisette brought is:
\[5 \text{ (apples)} + 8 \text{ (oranges)} = 13 \text{ fruits}\]
Adding the total number of fruits that both brought together gives us:
\[13 \text{ (Morisette's fruits)} + 14 \text{ (Kael's fruits)} = 27 \text{ fruits}\]

Therefore, the total number of fruits they have in total is \(\boxed{27}\).
