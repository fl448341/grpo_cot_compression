Problem (answer 27): Morisette and Kael were asked to bring fruits. Morisette brought 5 apples and 8 oranges, 
while Kael brought twice the amount of apples and half the number of oranges than Morisette. How many fruits do they have in total?

[brief prompt | 197 tok | correct]
To find the total number of fruits brought by Morisette and Kael, we need to calculate the number of each type of fruit each person brought and then sum them up.

1. **Morisette's Fruits:**
   - Apples: 5
   - Oranges: 8

2. **Kael's Fruits:**
   - Twice the amount of apples: \(2 \times 5 = 10\) apples
   - Half the number of oranges: \(\frac{8}{2} = 4\) oranges

3. **Total Fruits:**
   - Total apples: \(5 + 10 = 15\)
   - Total oranges: \(8 + 4 = 12\)

4. **Summing Up:**
   - Total fruits: \(15 + 12 = 27\)

Therefore, the total number of fruits they have is 27.
